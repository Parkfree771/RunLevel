풀 마라톤 등급별 주간 맞춤 훈련 프로그램

Weekly Customized Training Program for Full Marathon Runners by Grade
Based on the full marathon (42.195km) record data you provided, here is a one-week training plan to help runners of each grade achieve their next goal. As the full marathon is the longest of all long-distance challenges, gradual increases in training volume, strategic nutrition (carb-loading), and sufficient rest are paramount.

1. Weekly Plan for C+, C, D+, D Grade Runners Aiming for B, B+
Goal: To focus on building strong foundational fitness to complete 42.195km without injury and to develop confidence in long-distance running. (Target Time: 3:58:01 ~ 4:35:00)

Day	Workout Type	Workout Details	Training Goal & Tips
Monday	Rest or Cross-Training	Complete rest or 30-40 minutes of swimming, cycling.	🚴 Injury Prevention: Aid recovery with low-impact exercises for your joints after the weekend's long run.
Tuesday	Light Jog	Jog for 50-60 minutes at a comfortable pace.	😊 Maintain Consistency: Steadily build the foundational fitness required for long distances.
Wednesday	Steady-State Run	15-min warm-up
Main set: Run 8-10km at a slightly faster than comfortable pace.
15-min cool-down	🔥 Improve Endurance: Strengthen the "ability to run for a long time," which is the basis of a marathon.
Thursday	Rest	Complete rest.	🛌 Full Recovery: Allows your body to recharge between intense workouts and long runs.
Friday	Light Jog	Jog for 40 minutes at a very comfortable pace.	🍃 Conditioning: Loosen up your body to prepare for the weekend's key long run.
Saturday	Long Slow Distance (LSD)	Run 16-20km at a slow, conversational pace (increase by 1-2km weekly, with a reduced distance week after 3 weeks).	🏃‍♂️ Distance Adaptation: The most important workout. Develops the ability to use fat as an energy source.
Sunday	Rest	Complete rest.	☕️ Recharge: Store up energy for the next week's training and aid muscle growth.
2. Weekly Plan for B, B+ Grade Runners Aiming for S, A+, A
Goal: To move beyond a "Sub-4" (sub-4-hour finish) and enter the mid-3-hour range by focusing on improving adaptation to actual Marathon Pace (MP). (Target Time: 3:03:01 ~ 3:58:00)

Day	Workout Type	Workout Details	Training Goal & Tips
Monday	Rest	Complete rest.	🛌 Reset: Fully recover before starting a week of intense training.
Tuesday	Marathon Pace (MP) Run	15-min warm-up
Main set: Run 8-12km at your actual target marathon pace.
15-min cool-down	🎯 Race-Sense Training: Engrain the target pace into your body to develop efficient energy distribution.
Wednesday	Recovery Run	Jog for 50-60 minutes at a very comfortable pace.	🍃 Relieve Fatigue: Gently loosen up to relieve fatigue from yesterday's workout and aid blood circulation.
Thursday	Tempo Run	15-min warm-up
Main set: Run 7-9km at a "comfortably hard" pace.
15-min cool-down	🚀 Lactate Threshold Training: Develops the ability to sustain a hard pace, preventing your pace from dropping after 30km.
Friday	Rest	Complete rest.	🧘 Full Recharge: Store glycogen to prepare for the weekend's long run.
Saturday	Long Slow Distance (LSD)	Run for 22-28km at a comfortable pace.	🏞️ Core Endurance: Build the strong physical and mental foundation to endure the latter half of the marathon.
Sunday	Light Activity or Rest	Light walk, stretching, or complete rest.	😊 Flexible Finish: End the week by choosing an activity based on your condition.
3. Weekly Plan for S Grade Runners Aiming for SS
Goal: To approach an elite-level "Sub-3" (sub-3-hour finish) by increasing total training volume and maximizing the ability to maintain pace until the end of the race. (Target Time: Under 3:03:00)

Day	Workout Type	Workout Details	Training Goal & Tips
Monday	Recovery Run	Very light jog for under 60 minutes.	🍃 Active Recovery: An essential process to handle high training volume.
Tuesday	High-Intensity Intervals	20-min warm-up
Main set: (1.6km (1 mile) at 10k pace + 3 min jog) x 4-5 reps
15-min cool-down	💥 Break Speed Limits: Improve overall running economy (efficiency) to make marathon pace feel easier.
Wednesday	Mid-distance Steady Run	Run for 70-80 minutes at a comfortable pace.	🔥 Strengthen Steady-State: Maintain high training volume and consistently stimulate your aerobic capacity.
Thursday	Long Marathon Pace (MP) Run	15-min warm-up
Main set: Run 16-20km at your target marathon pace (MP).
15-min cool-down	🎯 The Ultimate Race-Specific Workout: Develops the ability to maintain pace when tired to overcome the '35km wall.'
Friday	Rest	Complete rest.	🛌 Supercompensation: A key time for your body to recover from micro-damage caused by intense training and become stronger.
Saturday	Long Slow Distance (LSD)	Run 30-35km at a comfortable pace (only until 3 weeks before the race).	marathon The Final Sharpening: The most important workout to check and complete all abilities for the marathon.
Sunday	Recovery Run or Rest	40-minute light jog or complete rest.	🧘‍♂️ Finish and Readjust: Carefully check your condition and prepare for the next week's training.
