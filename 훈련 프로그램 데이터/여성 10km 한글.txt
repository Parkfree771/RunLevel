Weekly Customized Training Program for Female 10km Runners by Grade
Based on the 10km record data for female runners you provided, here is a one-week training plan to help runners of each grade advance to the next level. For female runners, it's important to listen to your body and flexibly adjust training intensity, as your condition can vary with your menstrual cycle.

1. Weekly Plan for C+, C, D+, D Grade Runners Aiming for B, B+
Goal: To experience the joy of running consistently and to focus on building the foundational fitness to complete 10km without injury. (Target Time: 1:01:01 ~ 1:09:00)

Day	Workout Type	Workout Details	Training Goal & Tips
Monday	Long Slow Distance (LSD)	Run continuously for 40-50 minutes at a comfortable pace.	🏃‍♀️ Build Foundational Fitness: Maintain a conversational pace and focus on time, not distance.
Tuesday	Rest	Complete rest.	🛌 Muscle Recovery: Rest is as important as training.
Wednesday	Intro to Intervals	10-min warm-up
Main set: (2 min slightly fast + 3 min walk/jog) x 5 reps
10-min cool-down	⚡️ Improve Speed: Gently stimulate cardiovascular endurance to increase running speed.
Thursday	Rest or Light Activity	Complete rest or 30 minutes of yoga, Pilates.	🧘‍♀️ Active Recovery: Build core strength and flexibility to prevent injuries.
Friday	Light Jog	Jog for 30 minutes at a very comfortable pace.	😊 Form a Habit: Reduce the pressure of running and maintain consistency.
Saturday	Rest	Complete rest.	☕️ Recharge: Store up energy for the weekend's training.
Sunday	Cross-Training or Rest	40 minutes of cycling, swimming, hiking, etc., or rest.	🚴‍♀️ Injury Prevention: Use other muscles to complement your running and prevent injuries.
2. Weekly Plan for B, B+ Grade Runners Aiming for S, A+, A
Goal: To break the 1-hour barrier and focus on increasing training intensity and quality to achieve a time in the low-to-mid 50-minute range. (Target Time: 48:01 ~ 1:01:00)

Day	Workout Type	Workout Details	Training Goal & Tips
Monday	Rest	Complete rest.	🛌 Reset: Fully recover before starting the week's training.
Tuesday	Tempo Run	15-min warm-up
Main set: Maintain a "comfortably hard" pace for 20-25 minutes.
15-min cool-down	🔥 Enhance Endurance: Key to improving your time by developing the ability to sustain a hard pace for longer.
Wednesday	Recovery Run	Jog for 40 minutes at a very comfortable pace.	🍃 Relieve Fatigue: Gently loosen up to relieve fatigue from yesterday's workout.
Thursday	Interval Training	15-min warm-up
Main set: (1km faster than target pace + 3-min rest) x 3-4 reps
15-min cool-down	🚀 Boost Max Potential: This is a key workout to take your speed to the next level.
Friday	Rest	Complete rest.	🧘‍♀️ Full Recharge: Prepare for the weekend's long run.
Saturday	Long Run (LSD)	Run for 60-80 minutes at a comfortable pace.	🏞️ Build Confidence: Develop the physical and mental endurance for long distances.
Sunday	Light Activity or Rest	Light walk, stretching, or complete rest.	😊 Flexible Finish: End the week by choosing an activity based on your condition.
3. Weekly Plan for S Grade Runners Aiming for SS
Goal: To aim for an advanced time of under 48 minutes by maximizing race management skills and speed endurance. (Target Time: Under 48:00)

Day	Workout Type	Workout Details	Training Goal & Tips
Monday	Recovery Run	Jog for under 40 minutes at a very easy pace.	🍃 Active Recovery: Effectively eliminate fatigue from the weekend's training.
Tuesday	Race Pace Training	20-min warm-up
Main set: (2km at target race pace) x 3 reps, with 4-min rest between sets
15-min cool-down	🎯 Sharpen Race Sense: Perfectly adapt your body to the target pace and gain psychological confidence.
Wednesday	Recovery Run or Rest	30-minute light jog or complete rest.	🧘‍♀️ Condition Control: Get in top shape for the next high-intensity workout.
Thursday	High-Intensity Intervals	20-min warm-up
Main set: (400m all-out sprint + 400m jog) x 8-10 reps
15-min cool-down	💥 Break Speed Limits: Maximize your finishing kick ability and improve running efficiency.
Friday	Rest	Complete rest.	🛌 Supercompensation: A key time for muscles to become stronger through the cycle of damage and repair.
Saturday	Long Run + Acceleration	After a 10-12km steady run, run the last 1km at tempo pace.	📈 Race Simulation: Develop the ability to run fast even when tired, increasing competitiveness in the latter half of the race.
Sunday	Cross-Training or Rest	40-50 minutes of swimming, cycling, etc., or rest.	🏊‍♀️ Balanced Development: Use other muscles to prevent injuries and aid recovery.
